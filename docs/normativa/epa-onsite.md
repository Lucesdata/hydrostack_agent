# EPA Onsite Wastewater Treatment Standards (USA)

## Overview
EPA 625/R-06/003 "Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems Manual" provides the primary guidance for septic system design and installation in the United States. State and local health departments may have more stringent requirements. **Always verify with local authorities**.

## Design Flow Requirements

### Domestic Wastewater
| Property Type                | Flow Rate (GPD) |
|------------------------------|-----------------|
| Single-family residence      | 75 GPD/bedroom  |
| Multi-family (avg 3 bed)     | 150-300 GPD/unit |
| Seasonal/vacation home       | 50 GPD/bedroom  |
| Small commercial             | 100-200 GPD/employee |

**Typical conversions:**
- 75 GPD/bedroom ≈ 285 L/day (≈95 L/person/day)
- Single family (3 beds): ~225 GPD/day ≈ 850 L/day

## Septic Tank Sizing

### Minimum Tank Volume
- **1,000 gallons (3,785 L)**: absolute minimum for any system
- **Typical sizing rule**: 1,500 gal + 250 gal per bedroom above 3
  - 3 bedrooms: 1,500 gal
  - 4 bedrooms: 1,750 gal  
  - 5 bedrooms: 2,000 gal

### Tank Dimensions
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Minimum length | 4 ft (1.2 m) |
| Minimum width  | 3 ft (0.9 m)  |
| Minimum depth  | 4 ft (1.2 m)  |
| Hydraulic detention time (HDT) | 1.5–2.0 days @ standard flow |
| Length:Width ratio | 2:1 typical |

### Retention Time by Temperature
| Temperature | Retention Time |
|------------|----------------|
| ≥20°C (≥68°F) | 1.5 days |
| 10–19°C (50–66°F) | 2.0 days |
| <10°C (<50°F) | 2.5 days |

Higher retention time in cold climates ensures anaerobic digestion of solids.

## Soil Absorption System (Leach Field)

### Soil Percolation Rates (ASTM D6391)
| Soil Type | Percolation Rate (min/inch) | Infiltration Rate (GPD/sq.ft.) |
|-----------|----------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Gravel    | 0.5–2.0 | 80–120 |
| Sand      | 2.0–4.0 | 60–80  |
| Sandy loam | 4.0–8.0 | 40–60 |
| Loam      | 8.0–16.0 | 20–40 |
| Silt loam | 16.0–32.0 | 10–20 |
| Clay loam | >32.0 | 5–10 |
| Clay (pure) | >60 | Not suitable |

**Note:** Percolation test is the only acceptable method for site-specific determination.

### Minimum Leach Field Size
```
Area (sq.ft.) = Design Flow (GPD) / Infiltration Rate (GPD/sq.ft.)
```

### Depth Requirements
- **Typical depth:** 18–36 inches (0.45–0.9 m)
- **Minimum distance to seasonal water table:** 2 ft (0.6 m) minimum
- **Minimum distance to bedrock:** 3 ft (0.9 m)

## Setback Distances

### Minimum Distances (Typical — Verify with Local Authority)
| Feature | Minimum Distance |
|---------|-----------------|
| Water wells / springs | 50–100 ft (15–30 m) |
| Property line | 10 ft (3 m) |
| Building foundations | 10 ft (3 m) |
| Surface water bodies | 50 ft (15 m) |
| Streams / rivers | 75 ft (23 m) |
| Public water supply | 100 ft (30 m) |

**Important:** Requirements vary significantly by state and local jurisdiction. Some states require 100+ feet from wells. Always consult local health department.

## Design Checks

### Organic Volumetric Loading
- **Maximum OVL:** 0.30 kg BOD₅/m³·day (≈ 0.015 lbs/1,000 gal·day)
- **If exceeded:** Tank becomes overloaded; anaerobic digestion fails

### Sludge Accumulation
- **Typical rate:** 0.5–1.0 gal/person·year (for residential)
- **Recommended pumping interval:** 3–5 years for typical family
- **Visual inspection:** If sludge exceeds 50% tank height, pump immediately

## Treatment Levels

### Primary Treatment (Septic Tank Alone)
- BOD₅ reduction: ~40%
- TSS reduction: ~60%
- Effluent quality: Poor; suitable only for well-drained soils
- **Note:** NOT acceptable for mounded systems or high-risk sites

### Secondary/Polishing Treatment
Required in high-risk areas:
- Close to water supply
- High groundwater
- Poor soil infiltration
- Nearby surface water

Options:
- Constructed wetland
- Sand filter
- Peat filter
- Advanced treatment unit

## Installation Requirements

### Tank Construction
- **Material:** Precast concrete, fiberglass, HDPE, or masonry
- **Structural integrity:** Must withstand soil and hydrostatic loads
- **Capacity tolerance:** ±5% of design volume

### Access & Maintenance
- **Inspection access:** Minimum one 24-inch (0.6 m) diameter access port per compartment
- **Cleanout access:** Must allow pumping truck access
- **Risers:** Recommended for easy locating (buried 6–12 inches)

### Piping
- **Inlet pipe:** Submerged minimum 6 inches (0.15 m) below water surface
- **Outlet pipe:** Submerged minimum 12 inches (0.30 m) below outlet baffle
- **Minimum pipe slope:** 1/8 inch per foot (1:96)
- **Diameter:** Minimum 3–4 inches; sized for peak hourly flow

### Ventilation
- **Vent pipes:** Minimum 2 inches diameter per compartment
- **Height:** Extend minimum 12 inches above grade and 10 feet (3 m) from windows/doors
- **Screening:** Install insect-proof screen

## Operation & Maintenance

### Required Practices
✓ Pump tank every 3–5 years (more frequent if system is heavily used)  
✓ Inspect annually for cracks, seepage, and settlement  
✓ Keep detailed records of all maintenance  
✓ Never use chemical tank "cleaners" or enzyme additives  
✓ Avoid introducing grease, oils, harsh chemicals, or non-biodegradables  

### Prohibited Items
✗ Cooking grease and oils (solidify; clog system)  
✗ Chemical drain cleaners, bleach, degreasers (kill bacteria)  
✗ Non-biodegradable wipes, diapers, hygiene products  
✗ Pesticides, paint, solvents, pharmaceuticals in large quantities  
✗ Garbage disposal waste  
✗ High-volume water use (affects retention time)
