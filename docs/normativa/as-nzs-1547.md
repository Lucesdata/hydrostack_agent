# AS/NZS 1547: On-site Domestic Wastewater Management (Australia & New Zealand)

## Overview
AS/NZS 1547:2012 specifies design and installation requirements for on-site domestic wastewater treatment systems in Australia and New Zealand. This standard applies to systems serving up to 50 Person Equivalent (PE). Local council approval and EPA regulations may be more stringent.

## Wastewater Generation

### Domestic Design Flow (Per Person Equivalent)
| Occupancy Type | Flow Rate |
|----------------|-----------|
| Residential dwelling (per person) | 200 L/person/day |
| Holiday/seasonal occupancy | 100 L/person/day |
| Transient occupancy (caravan park) | 150 L/person/day |
| Small commercial | 80–100 L/employee/day |

### Conversion to Imperial
- 200 L/person/day ≈ 53 GPD/person
- Typical 4-person household: ~800 L/day (≈211 GPD)

## Septic Tank Design

### Minimum Tank Capacity
| Persons Equivalent (PE) | Minimum Volume |
|------------------------|----------------|
| Up to 5 PE | 1,500 L (400 gal) |
| 6–10 PE | 2,000 L (530 gal) |
| 11–15 PE | 2,500 L (660 gal) |
| 16–20 PE | 3,000 L (800 gal) |
| Each additional person | +200 L (+53 gal) |

**Note:** Minimum **must not be less than 1,500 L** regardless of occupancy.

### Tank Geometry Requirements
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Minimum length | 1.5 m (4.9 ft) |
| Minimum width | 0.9 m (3 ft) |
| Minimum depth | 1.2 m effective depth (4 ft) |
| Length:Width ratio | 1.5:1 to 3:1 |
| Hydraulic detention time (HRT) @ design flow | 1.5–2.0 days |
| Freeboard | Minimum 0.3 m (1 ft) above max WL |

### Temperature Adjustments
| Annual Mean Temperature | Retention Time |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| ≥15°C (≥59°F) | 1.5 days |
| 10–14°C (50–57°F) | 2.0 days |
| 5–9°C (41–48°F) | 2.5 days |
| <5°C (<41°F) | 3.0 days |

Higher RT in cooler climates ensures complete anaerobic digestion.

## Soil Assessment & Infiltration Systems

### Percolation Testing (AS/NZS 1547 Appendix A)
Percolation test measures soil's ability to absorb water.

| Soil Description | Percolation Time (min/25mm) | Infiltration Rate (mm/day) |
|------------------|----------------------------|---------------------------|
| Coarse sand / gravel | <2 | >40 |
| Medium sand | 2–5 | 20–40 |
| Fine sand | 5–15 | 10–20 |
| Sandy loam / Silt | 15–60 | 2–10 |
| Clay loam | 60–120 | <2 (marginal) |
| Clay (pure) | >120 | Not suitable |

**Critical:** Percolation test is mandatory before system design. Table values are **indicative only**.

### Leach Field (Absorption Trench) Sizing
```
Trench length (m) = Daily flow (L) / (Infiltration rate (mm/day) × effective trench width (mm))
```

**Typical effective width:** 0.5–0.6 m (accounting for soil saturation near perimeter)

### Trench Construction
- **Depth of perforated pipe:** 0.5–0.9 m below surface (typically 0.6 m)
- **Trench width:** 0.5–0.9 m (smaller width = longer trench needed)
- **Gravel bed beneath pipe:** Minimum 0.1 m (4 in)
- **Spacing between parallel trenches:** 2× depth of perforated pipe
- **Pipe diameter:** 100 mm (4 in) minimum; 150 mm (6 in) for larger flows
- **Pipe slope:** 1:200 minimum (to prevent sedimentation)

### Minimum Distances to Aquatic Environments
| Feature | Minimum Distance |
|---------|-----------------|
| Water supply borehole | 30 m (100 ft) |
| Public water main | 10 m (33 ft) |
| Dwelling / building | 2 m (6.5 ft) |
| Property boundary | 1 m (3.3 ft) |
| Surface watercourse | 30 m (100 ft); 50 m if high-risk |
| Stormwater drain | 2 m (6.5 ft) |
| Seasonal water table | Minimum 0.6 m (2 ft) clearance |

## Tank Construction

### Materials (AS/NZS 1547:2012)
- **Precast concrete:** Compliant to AS/NZS standards
- **Fiberglass:** Engineered GRP tanks with UV/chemical resistance
- **Plastic (HDPE/MDPE):** For systems up to 3,000 L
- **Brick/block/stone:** Only if properly sealed (not recommended)

### Design Safety
- **Structural design:** Minimum factor of safety 1.5 against buoyancy
- **Wall thickness (concrete):** Minimum 75–100 mm
- **Base thickness:** Minimum 100–150 mm (depth-dependent)
- **Watertightness:** No seepage or infiltration from ground

## Access & Inspection

### Requirements
- **Access cover:** Minimum 500 mm diameter per compartment
- **Cover type:** Concrete or composite (lockable, child-proof)
- **Riser tube:** Recommended to bring access above ground level
- **Multiple compartments:** Access required to each chamber

### Sludge Level Monitoring
- Annual visual inspection recommended
- Pump if sludge ≥ 50% tank volume OR when performance deteriorates

## Secondary Treatment Options

### When Required
- Soil unsuitable for direct infiltration (poor percolation)
- High groundwater table
- Proximity to sensitive waters
- Seasonal use but long-term permanent residence expected

### Treatment Technologies
1. **Constructed Wetland (CW)** – 0.8–1.5 m² per PE, very reliable
2. **Sand Filter (SF)** – 0.3–0.5 m² per PE, requires regular backwashing
3. **Mound System** – if water table high or poor soil
4. **Package Treatment Unit (PTU)** – mechanical/biological advanced treatment (requires power)

All secondary systems must be designed per AS/NZS 1547 Appendix C.

## Pipe Installation Standards

### Inlet Pipe
- **Entry method:** Submerged 0.3 m (1 ft) below water surface via tee or baffle
- **Pipe slope:** Minimum 1:40 (prevents sedimentation)
- **Diameter:** 100 mm minimum (sized for peak flow)

### Outlet Pipe
- **Submersion depth:** 0.4 m minimum below lowest outlet baffle
- **Pipe orientation:** Draws from deepest part of tank  
- **Slope to field:** Minimum 1:200 (prevents backpressure)
- **Diameter:** 100 mm minimum

### Ventilation
- **Vent pipes:** Minimum 100 mm (4 in) diameter
- **Number:** One per tank section/chamber
- **Height above ground:** Minimum 0.3 m (1 ft) or 3 m from windows
- **Termination:** Mosquito-proof mesh screen

## Operation & Maintenance Standards

### Pumping Schedule
| System Type | Typical Interval |
|------------|-----------------|
| Residential septic tank | Every 3–4 years |
| High-use household | Every 2–3 years |
| After secondary treatment | 1–2 years |
| Commercial/institutional | 6–12 months |

**Trigger for early pumping:** When sludge occupies >30% tank volume.

### Maintenance Record Keeping
Keep documented records of:
✓ System installation details (date, capacity, design)  
✓ All pumping/emptying events (dates, volume, contractor)  
✓ Inspections and observations (visual checks, photos)  
✓ Repairs or modifications  
✓ Any system failures or issues  

### Good Operating Practices
✓ Use only licensed waste management contractors  
✓ Have system inspected annually by qualified technician  
✓ Maintain 1–2 m clearance around tank/field (no vehicles)  
✓ Keep plant roots away from pipes  
✓ Monitor water usage (high use = more frequent pumping)  

### Strictly Prohibited
✗ Chemical tank cleaners or enzyme additives (damage biomass)  
✗ Cooking grease and oils (solidify; clog pipes)  
✗ Non-biodegradable items (wipes, feminine hygiene, diapers)  
✗ Bleach, disinfectants, degreasers in large quantities  
✗ Paint, solvents, petroleum products  
✗ Large quantities of medications or antibiotics  
✗ Food scraps (unless triturator is installed)
